What is Exploratory Data Analysis?

  • Pioneered by Tukey
  • Detective work on your data
  • An attitude towards data, not just techniques
  • ‘Find patterns, reveal structure, and make tenative model assessments (Behrens)’

Being a good analyst requires you to look at the data and notice the weird things about it, and follow the thread to the end.

Why EDA and Visualization?

  • We need to become aware of patterns in the data.
  • Sometimes these patterns are desirable:
    • Associations
  • Sometimes they are not desirable:
    • Experimental Artefacts

Visualization is good for exploring data because we are really good at evaluating things visually.

Statistical Testing on Proportions

Now let’s talk about statistics. We have discovered that there is a difference in the proportions of alive to dead people between smokers and non-smokers. So how likely is this a random result?

We can assess whether the difference between the proportion of deaths is statistically significant between smokers and non-smokers.

Our test statistic is known as the Z-statistic.

Our null hypothesis and alternative hypothesis

What we call our null hypothesis represents what we think of results that are the result of complete random chance.

Our null hypothesis for proportions is there is no difference between our two ratios. To make things concrete:

Null hypothesis (Ho): No difference between proportion of deaths between smokers and non smokers

Alternative hypothesis (Ha): there is a difference between the proportion between smokers and non-smokers.

Remember the case where there was no association between smoking and death?

In statistics we model randomness

Every statistic has an underlying model of randomness. Our random model is based on the case with no association.

For the test of proportions we are asking, how likely is the proportion difference between smokers and non-smokers is due to a completely random process?

In other words, how rare is our outcome compared to a world of random outcomes? This world of outcomes is known as the null distribution.

For our test of proportions, the appropriate null distribution (or random model) is called a Chi-Square Distribution. We won’t explore all of the parameters, but it represents a random process for which there is no association between variables.

P-values

There is a straightforward interpretation to the p-value, and it has to do with how unique or rare our case is compared to our distribution of randomly generated cases.

\[\frac{# null}{# Total case}\]

So the p-value is interpreted as the probability that we will see a random case with the same exact configuration.

How does our test statistic increase with N?

One thing our test statistic is dependent on is n, the total number of subjects in the study.
